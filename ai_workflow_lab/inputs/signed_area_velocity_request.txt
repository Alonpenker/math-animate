Topic: Displacement from signed area under a velocity-time graph

Misconceptions:
- Students may count total shaded area as displacement even when velocity is negative.
- Students may think crossing the time axis means the object stops permanently.
- Students may confuse speed, velocity, distance traveled, and displacement.

Constraints:
- Use a velocity-time graph with one region above the axis and one region below the axis.
- Shade positive area and negative area in different colors, then combine them to show net displacement.
- Make clear that the graph is not a position-time graph by showing a small moving object whose position changes according to the signed areas.
- Include a brief comparison between total distance traveled and net displacement.
- Avoid calculus notation until the final summary, and keep the lesson accessible for a first introduction to area under a graph.

Examples:
- v(t) = 2 from t = 0 to 3
- v(t) = -1 from t = 3 to 7
- displacement = 6 - 4 = 2
- distance traveled = 6 + 4 = 10

Number of scenes: 3
