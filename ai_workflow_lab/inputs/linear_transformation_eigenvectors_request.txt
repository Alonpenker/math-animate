Topic: Eigenvectors of a 2D matrix as directions that do not turn

Misconceptions:
- Students may think multiplying by a matrix only changes vector length, not direction.
- Students may think every vector that gets longer is an eigenvector.
- Students may confuse an eigenvalue with the final coordinates of the transformed vector.

Constraints:
- Start with the matrix as a rule that transforms the whole plane, not as a row-by-column multiplication exercise.
- Show several arrows from the origin under the same transformation, then contrast a turning vector with a vector that stays on its original line.
- Include a lightly drawn unit square or grid cell deforming into a parallelogram to make the plane transformation visible.
- Derive one eigenvector visually before showing the algebraic check.
- Use minimal on-screen text and keep the notation suitable for an introductory linear algebra lesson.

Examples:
- A = [[2, 1], [1, 2]]
- v = [1, 1] becomes [3, 3]
- w = [1, 0] becomes [2, 1]

Number of scenes: 3
